Project Proposal: Hello My Name Is

Data Domain

Names are among the most integral parts of people’s identities. Yet, for various reasons, people change them once or several times throughout their lives. For some, a new name accompanies a transition in identity. For others, it’s a means of adapting to a new culture or country. For others still, it’s a necessary step to ensure safety and privacy. Regardless of the reason, people should feel at home in their names.

Problem Space

The problem is that people need an online space to search for, organize, and compare names. However, most existing name-related online tools are designed for a) soon-to-be parents naming their children or b) storytellers naming their fictional characters. This does not fully address the considerations of people seeking to add to or replace their own existing name. For example, someone born outside of the US may be looking for names with a similar sound and meaning as the name in their natural language. Additionally, a transperson might want a name popular during the year they were born, but with a different gender-association than their assigned name.

Existing Solutions

Currently, many of the available resources specifically for people changing their own name are online community threads. Some redirect people to existing name search resources, such as this Reddit post. Unfortunately, this does not fully solve the problem because resource repositories like these still redirect people primarily to baby name and fantasy name generator websites. Other threads, like this Quora post for Chinese-to-English names, directly ask for name advice. While this is helpful on a local level, it is not sufficiently scalable to support a large user base. Also worth mentioning are “name-swap” resources for transpeople, like this tumblr blog. But, again, these lack user personalization and scalability.

App description

3.What will the users do with the web app? What kind of interaction are you expecting to provide?

Users will need to create their own account for our app and they will need to log in before using our app. There are several ways users can interact with our app. User can search a name directly. When they hover their mouse on the name they want to look up, they can view information relavent to the name such as gender, origin, and meaning, etc. For users don't have a name in mind but want to find one, they are allowed to filter for a name in the app's database based on their preferences. They can filter through categories such as gender, origin, and spelling, etc. to find a name they want. Users are also allowed to bookmark names and create a list of names they want and view it when they want.

4. How will using this app help to solve your chosen problem? That is, how is it a solution to the problem you described?

The sign up and log in feature ensures safety and privavy for users since their information will be secured and not access by other people. Since we create catergories like gender, meaning, origin, spelling, etc. for filtering, users can find a name that target their needs. Meanwhile, it supports users from different social groups. Groups such as immigrants and parents can find a meaningful name they want based on their preferences through our app. The bookmark function makes it easier for users to access and compare the names they want though different ways. Therefore provides a convenient way for users to choose the name they want. Since our app has features that appeal users needs, we provide a way that maximize users freedom and ownership.
